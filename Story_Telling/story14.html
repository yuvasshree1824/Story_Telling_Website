The Hardworking Potter

Theme: Dignity of Labor

Kural: Labor, however humble, is honorable. (Kural 214)

Ramu was a village potter. He worked from dawn till dusk, molding clay into beautiful pots. Though poor, he never begged or complained. His hands were always covered in clay, and his spirit full of pride in his craft.

Children admired his skills, and elders respected his dedication. One day, a rich man mocked him, saying, “Why work so hard for so little?” Ramu replied, “I may earn little, but I eat with dignity and sleep with peace.”

Eventually, a festival brought great demand for Ramu’s pots. His stall became the busiest in the village. Even the same rich man returned, now buying pots and offering praise.

Ramu's story spread, reminding people that no job is small when done with honesty and dedication.

Moral: Hard work earns respect and success.
