The Arrogant Scholar

Theme: Humility

Kural: Humility is the true ornament of the learned. (Kural 408)

Once, a famous scholar named Somu visited a village to deliver a lecture. People gathered to hear him speak, but Somu was proud of his knowledge and often mocked others. During a discussion, he belittled a simple farmer for not knowing scriptures.

The farmer calmly stood up and quoted a Thirukkural about humility: “Learning is not in knowing, but in being gentle and respectful.” The crowd clapped. Somu was stunned that a man without formal education could speak with such wisdom.

Feeling embarrassed, Somu asked the farmer where he learned that. The farmer replied, “From life and from elders who lived with character.” The scholar realized he had mistaken pride for intelligence.

From that day, Somu began to speak more humbly and listen more carefully. He learned that true knowledge makes a person kind, not arrogant.

Moral Knowledge without humility is hollow.
