The Thirsty Traveler

Theme: Helping Strangers

Kural: Hospitality to even a stranger is virtue.(Kural 81)

On a scorching summer day, a traveler wandered through a desert, exhausted and thirsty. He knocked on many doors but found none open—until he reached a small hut where an old woman lived. She welcomed him warmly and gave him her last pot of drinking water.

The traveler, overwhelmed with gratitude, thanked her and continued his journey. Years passed, and the old woman forgot about the incident. One day, her son fell seriously ill in a faraway town and was rushed to a hospital.

The doctor who treated him recognized the family name. It was the same traveler she had helped years ago—now a well-known physician. He treated the boy with great care and refused any payment.

When the old woman learned the truth, she smiled with tears in her eyes. Her small act of kindness had returned to her in an unexpected and beautiful way.

Moral: Help others without expecting anything.
