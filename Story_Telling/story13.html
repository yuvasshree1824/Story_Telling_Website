The Brave Girl

Theme: Courage

Kural: Fear is for those who do wrong. The righteous fear nothing. (Kural 321)

In a small school, a quiet girl named Kavya noticed a group of students bullying a boy for wearing torn clothes. While others stayed silent, she stepped forward and said, “Leave him alone. Kindness costs nothing.”

The bullies laughed, but Kavya did not step back. She stood in front of the boy until a teacher arrived. The incident spread, and soon everyone praised her courage. The boy thanked her, saying, “No one has ever stood up for me before.”

Later, Kavya learned that the boy’s father was a war veteran and couldn’t work due to injuries. She organized a small donation drive among classmates to help the family. The boy’s life changed, and they became close friends.

Kavya's bravery reminded everyone that doing the right thing takes heart—not size, strength, or age.

Moral: True courage protects the weak.
