The Sharing Tree

Theme: Generosity

Kural: Like the earth that bears those who dig it, the wise give without anger. (Kural 987)

At the edge of a quiet village stood a big mango tree. Children would often throw stones at it to knock down ripe fruits. The tree never resisted, never stopped giving. Season after season, it continued to offer shade and sweet mangoes to all.

One day, a traveler saw a boy throw a stone and asked, “Why harm the tree that feeds you?” The boy shrugged, “It’s just a tree.” The traveler, saddened, told the village elders. Realizing their mistake, the villagers built a small fence around the tree to protect it.

They began to water it regularly and taught their children to treat it kindly. The tree kept giving, its branches heavy with fruit, its roots strong in the soil of kindness. It became a symbol of silent generosity.

The village prospered around it. People gathered in its shade for meetings and rest. The tree, though silent, reminded everyone that the greatest hearts give without asking anything in return.

Moral True greatness lies in giving without expecting.
